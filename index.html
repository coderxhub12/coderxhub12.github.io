History of Baguio City

Introduction

Baguio City, known as the "Summer Capital of the Philippines," is a city located in the northern region of the country. It was established as a hill station by the Americans in the early 1900s.

Early History

The area was originally inhabited by the Ibaloi and other indigenous groups. They utilized the rich natural resources and lived harmoniously with the land.

American Colonial Period

In 1903, Baguio was chosen by the American colonial government as a hill station due to its cool climate. They developed infrastructure and established the city layout, which included parks and public buildings.

World War II

During World War II, Baguio was occupied by Japanese forces. The city suffered significant damage, but it was liberated by American and Filipino forces in 1945.

Post-War Development

After the war, Baguio rapidly grew in population and importance, becoming a major tourist destination and cultural center.
